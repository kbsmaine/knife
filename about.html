THE HOLLOW

Where These Blades Come From.

Greyhollow is less "brand" and more badly lit workbench—steel dust, grinder noise, and paperwork stained in cutting oil.

ORIGIN

Born Between Range Days & Ruined Buildings.

Greyhollow Steelworks grew out of a frustration with knives that looked tactical but folded under actual use. The first patterns were built in a garage that shook every time the grinder spun up.

Instead of chasing trends, we chase feel: the way a handle locks when your hands are cold, the way a tip survives being buried into a door frame, the way a sheath sits when you bail out of a truck.

ETHOS

Rules of the Hollow.

  • No safe queens. If a blade ends up in a glass case, that's your choice, not our design.
  • No pretend steels. Specs are real, heat treat is honest, and geometry is tuned for work.
  • Small runs. We'd rather make fewer knives that get beat up than stacks of wall-hangers.

FUTURE

What's Coming Next.

The roadmap is simple: tighten existing patterns, experiment with new grinds and steels, and build collaborations with people who actually run their gear dirty—range instructors, SAR teams, and the night shift crews that live in fluorescent flicker.

If you end up carrying a Greyhollow knife, we hope you forget it's there until the one second you need it.
